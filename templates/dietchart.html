Diet Chart for Sports Students

Hydration

Proper hydration is essential for optimal sports performance. Drink plenty of water throughout the day and especially before, during, and after workouts.

Supplementation

While a balanced diet should provide most of the nutrients athletes need, certain supplements may be beneficial. Consult with a healthcare professional before taking any supplements.

  • Protein powder
  • Creatine
  • BCAAs (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)

Meal Plans

Pre-Workout Meal

Consume a balanced meal containing carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats about 2-3 hours before your workout to fuel your training session.

  • Whole grain toast with almond butter
  • Greek yogurt with fruit
  • Chicken and vegetable stir-fry

Post-Workout Meal

Replenish glycogen stores and support muscle recovery by consuming a meal rich in carbohydrates and protein within 30-60 minutes after your workout.

  • Protein smoothie with banana and spinach
  • Quinoa salad with grilled chicken
  • Sweet potato and black bean burrito

Recovery Meal

Focus on nutrient-rich foods to aid in recovery and promote muscle repair. Include a balance of carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats.

  • Salmon with roasted vegetables
  • Whole grain pasta with marinara sauce and lean ground turkey
  • Vegetable and tofu stir-fry
